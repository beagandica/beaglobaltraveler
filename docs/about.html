📖 My story

The beginning
Born in Venezuela 🇻🇪
Grew up in a country that taught me resilience, warmth, and the best arepas in the world. My first taste of travel was family trips across Latin America.
The move
New chapter in the United States 🇺🇸
Moved to the US and dove headfirst into tech. Built a career as a Product Manager, learning to ship products and solve problems at scale.
The mission
Founded Nuevo Foundation 🤖
Started a nonprofit to teach kids (especially from underrepresented communities) how to code. Because if I can go from Venezuela to tech, others can too.
The obsession
Caught the travel bug 🌍
What started as "let me see a few countries" turned into a full-blown obsession. 74 countries later, I realized the best way to learn about the world is to show up and eat the food.
2024-2025
Africa, South America, and Oceania 🦁
Seychelles, Kenya, Tanzania, Madagascar, Mauritius. Then Brazil, Chile, Argentina, Paraguay, Uruguay. Australia and New Zealand next. 48 of 50 US states checked off.
Now
Building beaglobaltraveler ✈️
Turning years of travel into interactive city guides with real data, interactive maps, food picks, and honest recommendations. No sponsored content, no affiliate links, just real experiences.

💡 What drives this site

🗺️
Actually been there
Every city guide is based on my real visits. No SEO-farm content, no "top 10 things" copied from other blogs. I walked those streets.
🍜
Food first
I believe you understand a culture through its food. Every guide leads with restaurants, street food, and dishes you should not miss.
📊
Data, not vibes
Interactive maps with pinned locations, budget ranges, neighborhood breakdowns, and day-by-day itineraries. Built by a PM who loves structure.
🚫
Zero sponsored content
No affiliate links, no "this post was sponsored by", no cookie popups begging for your email. Just honest recommendations.
🌙
Built different
Dark theme, clean design, interactive maps. This is a travel site built by an engineer, not a WordPress template with stock photos.
🤝
Open source
The entire site is on GitHub. Want to see how it's built? Want to suggest a city? PRs welcome.
